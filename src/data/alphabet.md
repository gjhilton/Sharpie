During this era, the alphabet had 24 letters. _I_ and _J_ were the same letter, as were _U_ and _V_: In each case, two graphs - two characters - could be used to write the same letter. 'V' for example was more often used to begin a word, with the 'u' form preferred for the body of the word. By default, Sharpie asks you to identify 'I' and 'J' separately - effectivey a 26-leter alphabet. If this feels anachronistic however, you can also opt into...

{{ALPHABET_TOGGLE}}

When this option is enabled, if you are shown a 'J' and answer 'I', that answer will be accepted.
